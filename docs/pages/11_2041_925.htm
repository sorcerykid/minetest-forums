Re: [Modpack] Home Decor [git] [homedecor_modpack]

by VanessaE » Sat Apr 20, 2019 9:23 pm

BIG update: The main homedecor mod has been split into many smaller components, by item type (e.g. lighting, kitchen, bathroom, windows/window treatments, and so on). A big thanks goes to TenPlus1 for splitting the wardrobe itself into its own mod, as well.

All of the new mods that sprang forth from the big blob will all run with a minimum of dependencies, though each of them still needs the same stuff here or there as before, like basic_materials, or building_blocks, or some component of minetest_game such as dye or default, etc.

In most cases, the only Home Decor component the new mods absolutely need is homedecor_common, which provides all of the API stuff, all of the textures and models that are used by more than one new mod, and the translations and locale stuff (this last part will go away soon, in favor of 5.0.0 client-side localization).

To upgrade and keep all your content as you remember it, simply update Home Decor as usual, then enable the entire modpack in your world config. Other than a couple of minor texture changes, you shouldn't notice any difference. No content has been added or deleted, and the dependency tree should be approximately the same.

If you have some third-party mod that depends on the old homedecor blob, it will need its depends.txt updated to look for the correct, new mod. Most likely this will be a one-line change to that file, and it'll probably need to point to homedecor_common now, same as the new mods (I can't think of any outside mods that depend on it though).
