D00Med wrote:That is very cool!, but what could the sextant be used for?

The sextant was an old-school compass/gps that measured the angle between a celestial object and the horizon for navigation (it could also be used to pinpoint an approximate location, latitude and longitude, on nautical charts).

For the sextant to be useful you would need a map that listed the angles between a star and the horizon as well as the listing the time (hours, minutes, seconds) to determine position--longitude and latitude; however, it has been made obsolete by the compass and gps.

Like, Wuzzy said, it was included --- just because.
